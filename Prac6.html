Albert Einstein

1879 – 1955 | Theoretical Physicist, Nobel Laureate, and Humanitarian

About Einstein

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics). His work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science.

He received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, a pivotal step in the development of quantum theory. Beyond his scientific achievements, Einstein was a passionate advocate for peace, civil rights, and education.
